Novel Solutions to Wicked Problems

Embracing the Circular Economy isn't merely a solution for the UK - it's an empowering opportunity, a challenge, and a catalyst for innovation. Yet, its influence extends beyond the environmental.

It's also a key to unlocking economic potential and fostering a society that is prosperous and equitable in equal measure.

Yet, the journey to a circular economy isn't one we can walk alone. It requires the power of collective wisdom that spans across borders.

Novel Solutions to Wicked Problems

Why Global South?

Countries like India have grappled with unique social, economic, and environmental challenges, leading to the emergence of transformative solutions. The novel approaches, born out of resourcefulness and resilience and, often community-centred, hold invaluable insights that can be applied to Scotland's own wicked problems.

With an array of activities from field visits to collaborative learning workshops, you will uncover inspiration from a Global South context, engage directly with the communities, experts, and the change-makers driving the circular economy in Mumbai.

Programme in Mumbai, India

Over five days, you will make an extraordinary journey through Mumbai. Meet exceptional human beings, experience captivating moments, forge meaningful connections, and return equipped with insights to shape your future and of the organisation you are a part of.

Contact Noel Mathias to learn more
Days 1 - 4

Meet with award-winning Jyoti Mhapshekar and her 3000 women waste pickers - the ‘recycling warriors’ of Mumbai creating regenerative businesses. Visit their operationalsites, explore their business models and learn from their iterative approaches to create solutions at scale.

Interact with the ‘Clothing Man’ of India and listed by Forbes Magazine as one of India’s most powerful rural entrepreneurs, Anshu Gupta. Visit his work at Goonj -a sustainable economic model using urban under-utilized and surplus material as a tool to trigger all- round development.

Get to grips with the Indian ‘Jugaad’ and how the Paper Mills in India are using frugal innovation to optimise recycling with Tushar Shah. Also get to know the entrepreneurs who responded to the Covid crisis with beds made from recycled paperboard.

Meet the Pioneer of Microfinance in India, Aloysius Fernandez, and learn how India’s vibrant financial ecosystem has embraced micro-capital generating solutions alongside community wealth

Day 5

This one-day workshop is designed as a safe and reflective space to articulate and integrate the inspiration and insights from our journey together. As fellow travellers, we will together explore what can we bring back home to our work and lives.

You’ll gain a unique perspective on scale which only India can offer

You’ll think more deeply about the environment in which you live and possible micro-solutions we can adopt and adapt.

You’ll be better connected with leaders across the UK.

You’ll lead from a more inspired place after meeting with innovative leaders in Mumbai.

You won’t return the same person.

The immersive experience

Field Visits to operational sites providing an in-depth understanding of the challenges faced, innovativepractices employed, and the impact generated.

Interactive Sessions in dialogue with experts, ask questions, and explore the practical applications ofthe circular economy principles.

Collaborative learning activities and a peer earning approach to enable cross-pollination of ideas,fostering a rich learning environment and inspiring participants to adapt and apply successful modelsto their own contexts.
